About Me

Business-oriented Software Developer

I truly enjoy creating software solutions to solve business problems.

When I started in college and was studying Management Information Systems, I discovered that I had a natural knack for programming. I eventually switched to a Business Management major, but I had already caught the programming bug and decided to pursue software development as a career path.

I rose through the ranks from entry-level programmer analyst all the way to management. In my software development career, I had diverse experiences solving business problems in a variety of industries and for many different sorts of clients. As I transitioned into management roles, I had fewer opportunities to do hands-on coding work. I later realized that it was the hand-on programming that I enjoyed the most.

After a successful career in software development, I left the industry to pursue an entrepreneurial dream of being a self-employed futures/commodities derivatives trader. For years, I was a “day trader” type of trader. While very lucrative, the markets evolved into 24-hour, round-the-clock activity. As that occurred, I found that I did not enjoy the lifestyle of round-the-clock trading. So, I changed to become a long-term position trader. While I initially welcomed the schedule freedom that this change provided me, I soon realized that I had very little to do on a day-to-day basis.

With all the free time that I had, I decided to refactor the code base for the custom trading applications that I had developed to use Object-Oriented Design principles. I had a blast working with code again was reminded of how much I truly enjoyed hands-on programming projects. When I completed the refactoring project, I thought it would be fun to explore modern, software development technology stacks as an avocation.

As I worked my way through acquiring skills for some of the popular languages, frameworks, and tools for front-end and back-end web stacks, I began to wish to that I was still professionally involved in software development and working on challenging real-world projects. At first, I dismissed the idea of returning to the industry because I had been away for so long. But as I continued acquiring these skills, my desire to return grew exponentially. I eventually concluded that life is short and that there is no good reason to not do what I truly enjoying doing.

I am excited to be back!
